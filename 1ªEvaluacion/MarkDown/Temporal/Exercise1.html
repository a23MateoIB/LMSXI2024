Headgehogs by Mateo Iglesias

Introduction

Hedgehogs are famous for their prickly spines, which they have everywhere except on their face, legs and bellies. These cute critters depend on their spines for defense, both while they sleep and when they face enemies. By curling into a tight ball and tucking in their heads, tail and legs, they protect vulnerable parts of their body.

Fast hedgehog facts

  • Scientific name: Erinaceus
  • Family name: Erinaceidae
  • Classification: Mammal
  • IUCN status: Least concern
  • Lifespan (in wild): Around seven years
  • Weight: Up to 1.1kg
  • Body length: 14cm – 30cm
  • Top speed: 9.5km/h bursts
  • Diet: Carnivore
  • Habitat:
    • Grassland
    • hedgerows
    • woodland
    • meadows

Some more specific facts

  1. They are pretty widespread and sociable
  2. They are nightowls who don’t like the heat
  3. They are lactose intolerant!
  4. They are carnivores
  5. They may need spectacles but they make up for it elsewhere
  6. They would be great at triathlon, if you switched cycling for climbing
  7. Many Hedgehogs are killed unintentionally
